But progress will free us, right? It's been centuries since anyone had to worry about their clan succeeding in hunting food for the day and fear a mere cavity will kill us. Surely, we will get to a point where food and shelter costs nothing?

For some reason, things seem quite the opposite. Inflation is accepted to be normal, and Five Below has become a joke. How is this possible? How is McDonald's supposedly making continuous progress in their business, but can no longer make a burger for a dollar?

The situation is complex. I know it is more complicated than I realize. But there are two things I want to highlight as one of the greatest failures of our society.

## Infinite growth

Companies are expected to grow infinitely. I mean, look at the stock market. If Google announced that they expect their growth to stagnate, their stock will worth _nothing_ in an instant. Even though this doesn't change the fact that they are generating around **80 BILLION DOLLARS** in revenue **PER QUARTER**, it doesn't matter if they aren't making more in the next quarter. That's because the entire point of _investing_ is to see the company grow. If the company stops growing, well, then my money is better invested in a company that will.

To meet this insane expectation, instead of improving the product, companies just raise the price.

Real estate, health care, telecommunications - they all sell means of survival. And when you sell something like that, you can charge people lifetime amount of money and still have people pay for them. What choice do they have, anyway?

## The Function of The Government

The government exists to create a self-sufficient, independent society.
